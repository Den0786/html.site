Ahensan Emmanuel Presbyterian Church

Ahinsan Presby was established in in May,1970 with 5 members. members were meeting in classroom for sunday and week days mornuing devotion.
Some of the founding members are.

  1. Op. Damptey
  2. Obp.Baah Nketiah
  3. Op. Opoku Agyemang
  4. Op. B B Bonsu
  5. Op. B B O Addo

Op B B Bonsu was appointed as the caretaker for a period of two(2) years, and then went for Catechist training so he later became the catechist for the church for a period of 10yrs.
As the membership of the church was increasing, they decided to acquire a land to build their permanent structure. God been so good, the got their first land at Ahinsan but it was too far from the town as at that time. This stopped them form purchasing that land. Later, they got another which was closer and settled on that one. This new place was where the Guiness Ghana and Coca cola bottling company used to dump their broken bottles and waste product. But since they had no otehr option, they accepted it. Members dedicated their time and resources to clear the land and prepare it for proper building land. They built their first structure,but it collapsed after few months. They had to go back to the classroom again and worship there.
The youth in the church at that time have to carry chairs and drums on their head and sometimes on wooden track. They gathered money and came back to the land again and this time it stood firm till date. After 10years of its establishment, it was elevated from preaching point to congregation. And now it is called Emmanuel congregation Ahinsan Years later, Mr. Asiamah was appointed as the Catechist and he served for years. After him, the following catechist has served the church as Catechist.
  1. Catechist Mark Duffour
  2. Catechist Stephen Amoase
  3. Catechist Michael Osei Bonsu

The current Catechist is Cat. Francis-Godwin Adusei.
There has been numerous Ministers, but they were not permanent or residential. They include:
  1. Rev. Adu Boahen
  2. Rev. Sebi Otchere
  3. Rev. Akwasi Frimpong

The first resident Minister was Rev.Mary Owusu, she served as the minister in charge for two years.
She went on transfer after the two years and then came Rev. Yaw Barimah-Asamoah Boadu.
He served for 6 good years, The congregation can now boast of about 700 members, of which 250 are children between the age of 5-17.
In the year 2018, the General Assembly Council (GAC) during it's annual meeting decided to crop sub-district from districts that ae increasing in membership and ministers. Atonsu District was one of those districts, so they started looking for a congregation that will serve as the district capital Ahinsan and Bokro became the target, but later thet settled on Ahinsan. In September 8th, 2019, Ahinsan became a district on it's own. The district now have 9 congregations. With about 900 members. Rev Yaw Barimah-Asamoah Boadu became the first district minister and he served for two years.
The current minister is Rev Mark Appiah Amo. He doubled as the minister in charge for Ahinsan Emmanuel cong. and at the same time as the District minister of Ahinsan District.
